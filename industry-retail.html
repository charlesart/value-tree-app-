Industry Overview

Retail and consumer packaged goods (CPG) businesses face intense competition, thin margins, and rapidly evolving consumer preferences. Value creation requires disciplined execution across merchandising, operations, and supply chain while adapting to omnichannel dynamics.

Key Characteristics

  • Thin margins - Grocery 2-4%, specialty retail 8-12%, requiring operational excellence
  • Inventory intensity - Working capital management is critical to returns
  • Consumer volatility - Preferences shift quickly; agility is essential
  • Channel complexity - Omnichannel requires balancing DTC, wholesale, marketplace
  • Scale advantages - Purchasing power, marketing efficiency, real estate leverage

Common Pitfalls

Over-Expansion

Opening too many stores too quickly without proven unit economics. Ensure new store payback is well-understood before aggressive rollout.

E-commerce Profitability

Growing digital sales that destroy margin through shipping, fulfillment, and returns. Model true channel economics including last-mile costs.

Inventory Mismanagement

Over-buying leads to markdowns; under-buying leads to stockouts. Both destroy value. Invest in demand planning capabilities.

Brand Dilution

Excessive discounting or mass-market expansion can damage premium positioning. Protect brand equity while driving growth.

Consumer Trend Misses

Retail moves fast. Failing to adapt to changing consumer preferences (sustainability, DTC, etc.) can quickly erode relevance.
