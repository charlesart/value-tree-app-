Industry Overview

SaaS businesses benefit from recurring revenue, high gross margins, and operating leverage at scale. PE investors focus on accelerating growth, improving unit economics, and driving toward profitability for businesses that may have prioritized growth over efficiency.

Key Characteristics

  • Recurring revenue - Subscription model creates predictable, compounding revenue streams
  • High gross margins - Typically 70-85%+ after hosting and support costs
  • Negative churn potential - Expansion revenue can exceed lost revenue
  • Capital efficiency - Low marginal cost to serve additional customers
  • Network effects - Some products become more valuable with more users

Common Pitfalls

Cutting Too Deep on R&D

Reducing engineering spend can slow product velocity and create technical debt. Maintain competitive feature development while improving prioritization.

Churn Acceleration

Aggressive price increases or service changes can spike churn. Test incrementally and segment customers by price sensitivity.

Over-Indexing on Enterprise

Enterprise deals are attractive but extend sales cycles and require different capabilities. Ensure you have the organization to support it.

Key Person Dependencies

Founder-led sales or product vision creates risk. Build institutional knowledge and processes before transition.

Integration Complexity

M&A in SaaS requires careful product and customer migration planning. Technical integration can take 12-24 months.
